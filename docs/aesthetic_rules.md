### 2.2 Bigonial to Bizygomatic Width Ratio
**Definition**: The ratio of jaw width (measured at the gonion points) to cheekbone width (measured at the zygion points).

| Ratio | Classification |
|-------|---------------|
| > 85% | Wide jaw (can look blocky) |
| 80-85% | Strong, masculine jaw |
| **75-80%** | **Ideal** (defined but harmonious) |
| 70-75% | Slightly narrow jaw |
| < 70% | Narrow jaw (V-shaped face) |

**Impact**: This ratio determines the perceived strength of the jawline relative to facial width.

### 2.4 Midface Ratio
**Definition**: The distance from the pupils to the upper lip divided by the total face height.

| Ratio | Classification |
|-------|---------------|
| < 40% | Short midface (can look childlike) |
| 40-42% | Slightly short |
| **43-44%** | **Ideal** (Golden Ratio) |
| 45-47% | Slightly long |
| > 47% | Long midface (ages the face) |

**Impact**: The midface ratio significantly affects perceived age and attractiveness. A slightly shorter midface is generally more youthful.

## 3. Orthotropics Principles

### Forward Growth
- Proper tongue posture (mewing) promotes forward facial growth
- Forward growth creates:
  - Higher cheekbones
  - Wider palate
  - Stronger jaw
  - Better canthal tilt

## 4. Improvement Strategies

### Soft Maxxing (Non-surgical)
- Mewing (proper tongue posture)
- Chewing hard foods (gum, mastic)
- Weight loss (to define facial features)
- Skincare routine
- Posture improvement

### Hard Maxxing (Surgical)
- Rhinoplasty (nose reshaping)
- Genioplasty (chin advancement)
- Jaw implants
- Cheek implants
- Blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery)
- Canthoplasty (canthal tilt correction)
